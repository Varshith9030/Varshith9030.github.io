/about

Long-form context behind the work — how I got here, what I'm focused on, what I'm reading next.

I'm an offensive security practitioner currently interning as a Red Team Security Analyst at FireCompass, where I run end-to-end attack simulations against real client environments — reconnaissance through persistence, with reports that explain not just what broke but why and how to fix it.

The work I'm most interested in is the part that doesn't scale — the manual, creative chain-an-exploit-into-something-worse layer that automated scanners can't reach. But I also build tooling. Mega-Finder came out of frustration with stitching together half a dozen recon scripts for every engagement; it's now a single async tool that does that work in one pass.

Outside of work I'm finishing my B.Tech in Computer Science and Engineering (Honors) with a specialization in Cyber Security and Blockchain at Lovely Professional University, expected 2026. I'm certified in PJPT, CompTIA PenTest+, Security+, Network+, and Red Hat RH124.

What I'm trying to learn next: deeper Active Directory tradecraft (Kerberoasting chains, NTLM relay attacks, ADCS abuse), C2 framework internals, and writing custom tooling that's stealthier than the off-the-shelf options.

If you're hiring penetration testers, red teamers, or offensive security engineers — or you just want to talk about a finding — the contact box on the right is the fastest way to reach me.
